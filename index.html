Hey, I'm Naren Akash

I am an AI researcher. I think a lot about what it takes for AI systems to reason well, not just accurately but reliably. Things like staying grounded in evidence, being calibrated under uncertainty, and actually working in practice. I happen to focus on its applications in healthcare and medicine, where these capabilities stop being nice-to-haves.

I lead the AI and biomedical informatics research at the Institute of Intelligent Medicine at AIG Hospitals, where I'm also a founding member. It is the first dedicated AI center within a medical institute in India. I work with clinicians and data scientists to make care delivery actually better. Before this, I was at Microsoft Research exploring multimodal LLMs and retrieval for clinical chatbots, and built surgical video analysis tools with Sankara Eye Foundation.

I did both my Bachelor of Technology (Honours) and MS by Research at IIIT Hyderabad. Most of that time was at the Medical Image Analysis Lab, part of CVIT - Center for Visual Information Technology, the computer vision group. I tried a lot of things, all heavily AI and deep learning: medical imaging, diagnostic tools, visualization for medical education, and a bit of teaching and mentoring, whatever seemed interesting. Somewhere along the way, research became my thing. I remain a computer engineer at heart, with a deep appreciation for robust software and systems.

Beyond research, I actively participate in the broader medical imaging community. I've been involved with the MICCAI Student Board for over five years, serving as President for two years and counting.
